Shadows

Shadows are a way to add depth and dimension to your designs. They can be used to create a sense of hierarchy, draw attention to specific elements, or simply add a touch of style.

In this example, we will explore how to create shadows using CSS.

Box Shadow

The box-shadow property in CSS allows you to add shadows to elements. You can specify the horizontal and vertical offsets, blur radius, spread radius, and color of the shadow.

box-shadow: 10px 10px 5px #888888;This define the Horizintal-offset:10px;

This will create a shadow that is offset 10 pixels to the right and 10 pixels down from the element, with a blur radius of 5 pixels and a color of #888888.
